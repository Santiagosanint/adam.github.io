What is Spiritual Psychology?

Spiritual Psychology, as defined by the University of Santa Monica, is “the study and practice of the art and science of Conscious Awakening.” It views us as Divine Beings having a human experience, with life as a “school” for soul growth, healing, and evolution. It integrates the study of the Soul back into Psychology, blending traditional methods like Gestalt, Humanistic, and Positive Psychology with a spiritual perspective.

At its core, Spiritual Psychology teaches that each person holds the wisdom and tools to resolve challenges, find answers, and live authentically. Spiritual Counseling uses heart-centered listening, compassion, and diverse techniques to help clients release what no longer serves them, cultivate forgiveness, and manifest their fullest expression as loving, abundant, and soul-centered beings.

Meet Adam

Adam Douglas Heyes, M.A. is a multidisciplinary creative and business developer, working primarily with filmmakers, artists, authors, transformational teachers, and non-profit organizations.

Adam has worked with From the Heart Productions for over a decade, helping the organization and its clients with pitch decks, design, editing, film and event production, website development, marketing, crowdfunding course design, and fundraising. Adam manages several programs for From the Heart Clients including our LargoAI reports and our Grant Advance donor research system, and designed our new Fiscal Sponsorship Dashboard. He also designs Allyra fundraising pages and provides support to filmmakers and helps at From the Heart in many different ways.

Adam worked extensively with two award-winning From the Heart documentaries, Ground Operations about sustainable farming for veterans, and Take Back Your Power about the dangers of smart meters. He also recently worked as Production Coordinator for the short film Adult Life Lab.

Adam graduated Summa Cum Laude and Phi Beta Kappa from UCLA’s Honors Program with a degree in Psychology, and holds a Master’s Degree in Spiritual Psychology from the University of Santa Monica. He also runs a thriving heart-centered counseling practice in Seattle.
